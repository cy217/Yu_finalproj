Japanese Internment

An Introduction

After the attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941 American civilians and military officials alike were concerned about the loyalty of Japanese-Americans living in the United States. On February 19, 1942 Franklin D. Roosevelt signed Executive Order 9066, which led to the forced relocoation and incarceration of between 110,000 and 120,000 people of Japanese descent.
