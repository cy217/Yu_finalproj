Looking for something similar?

The National Museum of American History ran an exhibition titled "Righting a Wrong: Japanese Americans and World War II" to commemorate the 75th anniversary of Executive Order 9066, and more information about their exhibit can be found here.

The California Museum's longest running exhibit is "Uprooted! Japanese Americans During WWII" which surveys a century of Japanese Americans in Califorania, starting from incarceration through the 1980s, more information about their exhibit can be found here.

All photographs were from Ansel Adams's Photgraphs of Japanese-American Internment at Manzanar, and the narratives from the Veterans History Project.
